The vision

The vision of this school is to train missionaries who will go to the nations with the gospel of Jesus Christ. The primary focus of this training will not be on doctrine, but on ministry training. Students who participate in this training will be expected to become missionaries.

Curriculum

Students at Ukunda Missions School will undergo an intensive six month course of Study to prepare them to become missionaries. Each course of study will include practical application. Students will not only learn in the classroom, they will be able to practice what they have learned as they participate in ministry teams in the field.

School history

In 1989, two students, Vaughn Martin and Abednego Mbali Kanyia, were ministering together in Ukunda at the coast of Kenya. One night the Lord spoke to Vaughn and told him to purchase a piece of land at Ukunda. The Lord told Vaughn that one day Kenya would send many missionaries into the nations of the earth and that a missionary training center would be built upon that piece of land. So the land was bought, and many years passed. Vaughn became a missionary in Europe, and Mbali became a businessman. In 2012, while Vaughn was praying in Europe, the Lord confirmed the vision once again and instructed Vaughn to begin the school. Ukunda Missions School opened in September 2013. Kenyan missionaries specializing in Muslim evangelism are now being trained and sent from the school. You can read about their exploits at www.kenyamissionsmovement.blogspot.com.
